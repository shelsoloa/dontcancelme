00
Brand personality

Calm hands, sharp eyes.

We are the friend who reads your timeline at 3am so you don't have to. Protective, never preachy. We can be a little cheeky about cancel-culture anxiety — but we are dead serious about your back.

P1

Protective, not scary

We surface risk to calm people down, not freak them out. Severity colors inform; copy reassures.

P2

Confident & plain

Big grotesque type, short sentences, real numbers. No jargon, no fear-mongering, no dark patterns.

P3

Cheeky in the margins

Wit lives in microcopy and empty states — never in the moment someone is about to delete something.

01
Wordmark

The dot does the work.

Lowercase, tight tracking, one weight. The period in .me is the brand's atom — it doubles as the status dot, the scrubber, and the “clear” signal across the product.

03
The risk scale

Five levels. One language.

This is the spine of the product. Every flagged post gets a level; the same five colors appear on badges, meters, the dashboard, and rule outcomes. Never mix in colors outside this scale to express risk.

Detection categories map onto the scale — examples: profanity & aged takes trend Low–Medium, insensitive / NSFW trend Medium–High, slurs / hate / doxxing are always Critical.

08
Iconography & emoji

Line icons. Restrained emoji.

Outlined, 1.6px stroke, rounded joins — quiet enough to sit next to colored badges. Emoji is allowed (we're internet-native) but only in marketing, empty states and example posts — never as a functional control.

09
Voice & tone

We've got your back.

Talk like a calm friend who happens to be very good at this. Lead with reassurance, then the facts. Save the wit for the wins.

✓ Sounds like us

“Found 3 posts worth a second look. No rush — nothing's public-facing yet.”
“Cleaned up. Your timeline's looking a lot safer.”
“You're in control. We never delete without your okay.”
“Nothing risky here. Go touch grass. 🌱”

✕ Not us

“WARNING: Your account is at SEVERE RISK!!!”
“You posted something problematic.”
“Leverage AI-driven reputational risk mitigation.”
